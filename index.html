Research

Research Overview

My work connects multimodal learning, medical imaging education, provenance-aware workflows, synthetic agent systems, and secure AI infrastructure. Across these themes, I focus on structured, verifiable, and human-centered AI.

Multimodal Learning & LLMs

I develop multimodal learning methods and evaluation frameworks for medical imaging education. This includes slide-grounded understanding, lecture-aligned text ensembles, interactive avatar-driven teaching systems, and structured methods for improving reliability in AI-mediated educational workflows.

AI Safety & Synthetic Agent Systems

I study how agent societies behave under risky instructions, norm formation, and decentralized regulation. My work examines how LLM-based agents propagate, correct, and regulate behavior in synthetic social environments, with implications for governance and AI safety.

Workflow Auditability & Provenance

I am interested in making AI workflows more accountable through provenance, structured outputs, and risk-aware evaluation. This includes blockchain-anchored registration, traceable workflow design, and system-level methods for reproducibility and auditability.

Secure Data Systems & Applied ML

My earlier work spans secure multiparty data querying, blockchain-based systems, Bangla NLP, adversarial robustness, and applied machine learning. These projects continue to shape my approach to reliability, privacy, and trustworthy system design.
