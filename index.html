Research

Research Overview

My work connects multimodal learning, medical imaging education, provenance-aware workflows, synthetic agent systems, and secure AI infrastructure. Across these themes, I focus on structured, verifiable, and human-centered AI.

Multimodal Learning & LLMs

I develop multimodal learning methods and evaluation frameworks for medical imaging education, including slide-grounded understanding, lecture-aligned text ensembles, interactive avatar-driven teaching systems, and structured educational AI workflows.

AI Safety & Synthetic Agent Systems

I study risky instruction propagation, norm formation, and decentralized regulation in synthetic agent societies, using language-model agents to investigate safety, governance, and emergent corrective behavior.

Workflow Auditability & Provenance

I am interested in making AI workflows more accountable through provenance, structured outputs, and risk-aware evaluation, including blockchain-anchored registration and traceable workflow design.

Secure Data Systems & Applied ML

My earlier work spans secure multiparty queries, blockchain-based systems, Bangla NLP, adversarial robustness, and applied machine learning, all of which continue to shape my approach to reliability and trustworthy system design.

I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Science at Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute, advised by Prof. Ge Wang in the Wang-AXIS Lab. My research sits at the intersection of multimodal AI, medical imaging education, workflow auditability, provenance, and synthetic agent systems.
