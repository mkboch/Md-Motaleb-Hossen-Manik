Areas

Research Focus

My work connects multimodal learning, medical imaging education, workflow auditability, blockchain-based provenance, and synthetic agent systems. The common theme is structured, verifiable, and human-centered AI.

Multimodal Learning & LLMs

I designed MEDI-SLATE, a slide-lecture aligned text ensemble for medical imaging education, and MILU, a consensus ensemble benchmark for multimodal lecture understanding. I also contributed to ALIVE, a content-aware avatar-lecture interaction engine, together with SlideChain for semantic provenance and N-ReLU for stochastic activation design.

AI Safety & Synthetic Agent Systems

I developed OpenClaw and the Moltbook environment to study risky instruction propagation and norm enforcement in agent-only social systems. I also developed ADAPT, an AI-driven decentralized adaptive publishing framework, as part of a broader research agenda on regulation, auditability, and accountability in synthetic societies.

Secure Information Retrieval & Blockchain

My earlier work focused on user-centric secure skyline queries, blockchain-supported data management, academic credential verification, crime record storage, and medical data sharing using Hyperledger Fabric and IPFS. This background continues to shape my interest in provenance and trustworthy AI workflows.

Machine Learning & NLP

My prior work includes sentiment and emotion recognition for Bangla text, adversarial robustness for text classifiers, COVID-19-related vision and genomics applications, and other machine learning systems that helped shape my later work on evaluation and reliability.
